Energy2028, explained

A four-part series by AMELIA POLLARD and ZACH LEVITT

On January 29, 2019, Middlebury College announced an ambitious plan to combat the threat of climate change. The plan included four major commitments: transition the College to 100% renewable energy sources, significantly reduce energy consumption, divest the College's $1 billion endowment from fossil fuel investments and support new educational initiatives focused on climate change.

However, questions remain about how the College will achieve these goals, and what it will mean for its students, staff and faculty. Through this project, the Campus is committing to critically reporting on the progress of the College in meeting these goals, and offering students context, background and a deeper understanding of how this process will work.

We hope that you follow along with this series beginning on October 24.

Part I: October 24, 2019

DIVESTMENT

Why now? And what next? After years of student activism, the College agreed to cease new investment into fossil fuels and pull out of existing commitments over the next 15 years. We explore the factors that led the College to agree to this ambitious plan and the challenges moving forward.
